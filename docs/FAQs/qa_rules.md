# General Rules


## Basic Rules

**Q:** Can I place my deck and reveal my Leader card in any order?

**A:** No. After you and your opponent place your decks, you will then reveal your Leader cards to each other.

**Q:** Are there any rules regarding the order in which Life cards are placed?

**A:** Yes, there are.
Place the card at the top of your deck so that it is at the bottom of your Life cards.

**Q:** What happens to a Character when its power is reduced to 0 or less by an effect?

**A:** The Character remains on the field with a power of 0 or less.

**Q:** Can I transfer a DON!! card already given to a Character to another Character during the Main Phase?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** Can I play another Stage card if there is already a Stage card on my field?

**A:** Yes, you can. You will need to reveal the Stage card being played, pay the cost, trash the card already in the Stage area, and then place the new Stage card in the Stage area.

**Q:** Is there a limit to the number of cards I can have in my hand?

**A:** No, there is not.

**Q:** What happens if an effect that gives −10000 power is activated against a Character with 6000 power?

**A:** The Character's power becomes −4000.

**Q:** What happens if 1 DON!! card is given to a Character that currently has −4000 power?

**A:** The Character's power becomes −3000.

**Q:** Can a Character's cost be less than 0?

**A:** No, it cannot. When a Character's cost falls below 0, it is treated as 0.

**Q:** If a Character is on the field with negative cost and then returned to my hand, can I play that Character at negative cost?

**A:** No, you cannot. When a Character subject to some effects is moved from one area to another, it loses the effects it was subject to in the original area.

**Q:** Does 'with a type including "XX"' include cards with types such as "Former XX" and "XX Allies"?

**A:** Yes, it does.

**Q:** Can I use a card with 0 cost if I have no DON!! cards in my cost area?

**A:** Yes, you can.

**Q:** In the Refresh Phase, can I choose not to return a DON!! card and leave it as given to a card?

**A:** No, you must always return DON!! cards to the cost area.

**Q:** What happens if the Character that is attacking or the Character that is the target of an attack is moved to a different area during the Attack Step, Block Step, or Counter Step?

**A:** At the end of each step, if the Character that is attacking or is the target of an attack has moved to another area, the battle ends without proceeding to the next step.

**Q:** If I use a Character with −2000 power to attack my opponent's Character with −2000 power, who wins the battle?

**A:** Your Character with −2000 power will win the battle, and your opponent's Character will be K.O.'d. If the card you attacked was a Leader, your Character would also win the battle and you would deal 1 damage to your opponent.

**Q:** I won a battle by using a Character with 0 power to attack a Character with 0 power. Will my opponent's Character be K.O.'d?

**A:** Yes, your opponent's Character will be K.O.'d.


## Keywords

**Q:** Can a [DON!! x○] effect still be activated if the card has more than the specified number of DON!! cards?

**A:** Yes, the effect can still be activated.

**Q:** When returning DON!! cards to my DON!! deck according to a [DON!! −○:] effect, can I return DON!! cards that have already been given to my Characters or Leader?

**A:** Yes, you can. You can choose to return any DON!! cards from your Leader area, Stage area, Character area, or cost area.

**Q:** Can I activate Counters of Characters on the field like [Counter +1000]?

**A:** No, you can only activate the Counters of cards in your hand.

**Q:** Can I use a Counter to increase the power of a card not being attacked?

**A:** Yes, you can. However, the effect will end at the end of the current battle.

**Q:** Do I need to pay a cost to use a Character's Counter?

**A:** No, there is no cost for using a Character card's Counter.

**Q:** Can I activate an Event card's [Counter] effect during my Main Phase?

**A:** No, you cannot. You can only activate an Event card's [Counter] effect during the Counter Step of a battle in which your opponent has attacked.

**Q:** If I have 5 Characters and play another Character, when I trash a Character, will its [On K.O.] effect activate?

**A:** No, it will not. The trashed Character is not K.O.'d, but directly moved to your trash.

**Q:** Does [On K.O.] mean that this effect activates when the Character with this effect is K.O.'d?
Or does it mean that it activates when the Character with this effect K.O.'s an opponent's Character?

**A:** The effect activates when the Character with the effect is K.O.'d. The effect does not activate when the Character with the effect K.O.'s an opponent's Character.

**Q:** If there are cards of the same name with [Once Per Turn] effects, can I activate each of these cards' [Once Per Turn] effects during the same turn?

**A:** Yes, you can.

**Q:** Can I play a Character card with an [On Play] effect without activating this [On Play] effect?

**A:** No, you must activate the [On Play] effect whenever possible.
However, if the effect has a cost, you can choose not to pay that cost and play the card without activating the effect.

**Q:** Does [When Attacking] mean that this effect activates when the card with this effect attacks?
Does this effect also activate when another card without this effect attacks?

**A:** This effect activates when the card with this effect attacks. This effect does not activate when another card without this effect attacks.


## Keyword Effects

**Q:** Can I use 2 or more [Blocker] effects in a single battle?

**A:** No, you cannot. During a single Block Step, you can activate up to 1 [Blocker] effect.

**Q:** Can I activate the [Blocker] of a Character being attacked?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** When I activate a card's [Blocker], can I use a Counter to increase that card's power?

**A:** Yes, you can.

**Q:** When my opponent is attacking, if I have a [Blocker] Character, do I have to activate that [Blocker]?

**A:** No, you can choose not to activate a [Blocker].

**Q:** Can I activate the [Blocker] of a rested Character?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** Can I choose not to trash a Life card if I succeed in dealing damage using a Character with [Banish]?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** Can I choose to deal 1 damage if I succeed in dealing damage using a Character with [Double Attack]?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** When dealing 2 damage with [Double Attack], if this Character loses its [Double Attack] or leaves the field due to a Trigger effect from the initial damage, is the remaining damage no longer dealt?

**A:** No, the remaining damage is still dealt. Since the damage to be dealt is fixed at 2, 2 damage will be dealt even if the Character leaves the field or has their [Double Attack] effect disabled due to a [Trigger] effect or for any other reason.

**Q:** If my opponent has 1 Life card, can I win the game by using a [Double Attack] to deal 2 damage?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** Can I add a card to my hand without activating its [Trigger]?

**A:** Yes, you can.

**Q:** When I move a Life card with a [Trigger] due to an effect such as "Add 1 card from the top of your Life cards to your hand" or "Trash up to 1 card from the top of your Life cards", can I activate that [Trigger] effect?

**A:** No, you cannot.
[Trigger] effects can only be activated upon taking damage.


## Basic Rules

**Q:** If an effect states "up to" a specified number of cards, can I choose 0 cards?

**A:** Yes, you can choose 0 cards.

**Q:** Can I activate the effect of a card with a specified target if that target is not present at that time?

**A:** Yes, you can. However, nothing will happen in that case.

**Q:** Where an effect that states "during this turn" is activated and resolved by an [Activate: Main] or other effect, is that "during this turn" effect still valid during the turn if the card that activated that effect is moved to another area?

**A:** Yes, it is valid.

**Q:** When adding a Life card to my hand upon taking damage, the Event card had a [Trigger] effect so I activated that Event card's [Trigger] effect instead of adding it to my hand. In this case, will effects that state "when you activate an Event" be activated?

**A:** No, they will not.

**Q:** When text refers to 1 card from the top or bottom of the Life cards, what card is it referring to?

**A:** It is referring to the card at the very top or very bottom of the Life cards.

**Q:** When directed to place a card at the top or bottom of the Life cards, where should I place the card?

**A:** Place the card at the very top or very bottom of the Life cards. If there are no cards in the Life area, place the card in the Life area. The card must be placed face-down unless you are directed otherwise.

**Q:** When I add a card from my deck to my Life cards, can I or my opponent look at that card?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** Can I activate the Trigger of a card placed face-up in my Life area?

**A:** Yes, you can.

**Q:** During online regionals and other online matches, when a card effect allows me to look at my opponent's Life card(s), what is the proper procedure?

**A:** For upcoming English version online events only, this effect will be treated as "Look at up to 1 card from the top of your life cards, or your opponent reveals up to 1 card from the top of their life cards." in order to accommodate for the practical limitations of a webcam tournament.


## Advanced Rules

**Q:** Where an effect states "Look at up to 1 card from the top of your or your opponent's Life cards, and place it at the top or bottom of the Life cards.", can I look at a card from the top of my Life cards, and place it at the top or bottom of my opponent's Life cards?

**A:** No, you cannot. After looking at the Life card, place it at the top or bottom of that card's owner's Life cards.

**Q:** Where an effect states "Look at up to 1 card from the top of your or your opponent's Life cards, and place it at the top or bottom of the Life cards.", can I look at a card from the top of my opponent's Life cards, and place it at the top or bottom of my Life cards?

**A:** No, you cannot. After looking at the Life card, place it at the top or bottom of that card's owner's Life cards.


## Basic Rules

**Q:** Can DON!! cards given to Characters or Leaders, or DON!! cards in the DON!! deck be set as active or rested according to effects?

**A:** No, they cannot.


## Keyword Effects

**Q:** I have taken 2 damage from my opponent's Character with [Double Attack]. If the card from the first damage has a [Trigger], do I activate this [Trigger] effect before the second damage is dealt?

**A:** Yes, the [Trigger] is activated before the second damage is dealt. If you still have 1 or more Life cards left after you have activated the [Trigger] effect from the first damage, check a Life card according to the second damage.

**Q:** When I activate [OP03-074: Top Knot]'s [Trigger] effect "Activate this card's [Main] effect.", can I activate the [Main] effect without paying its "DON!! − 2" activation cost?

**A:** No, you cannot.

**Q:** Which activates first, the [When Attacking] effect of a card that has attacked on my opponent's turn or my [On Your Opponent's Attack] effect?

**A:** Your opponent's [When Attacking] efect activates first.

**Q:** If I have a card with an [On Your Opponent's Attack] effect on my field and my opponent targets a different card to attack, can I still activate the [On Your Opponent's Attack] effect?

**A:** Yes, you can.


## Advanced Rules

**Q:** If a Leader with the effect "This Leader is treated as a card with all card names, types, and attributes." attacks a Character with the effect "This Character cannot be K.O.'d in battle by <Slash> attribute cards.", can that Leader K.O. that Character in battle?

**A:** No, it cannot.

**Q:** Can a Leader with the effect "This Leader is treated as a card with all card names, types, and attributes." fulfill the effect condition "If your Leader has the {XX} type"?

**A:** Yes, it can.

**Q:** Can a Leader with the effect "This Leader is treated as a card with all card names, types, and attributes." fulfill the effect condition "If your Leader's type includes "XX""?

**A:** Yes, it can.

**Q:** If my Leader has the effect "This Leader is treated as a card with all card names, types, and attributes.", can my [OP04-005 Kung Fu Jugon] on the field gain [Blocker] according to the effect "If you have a [Kung Fu Jugon] other than this Character, this Character gains [Blocker]."?

**A:** Yes, it can.

**Q:** When an effect states "You may rest 1 of your DON!! cards:", is it equivalent to the effect "➀ (You may rest the specified number of DON!! cards in your cost area.):"?

**A:** Yes, both refer to activation costs that can be paid by resting one of the active DON!! cards in your cost area.

**Q:** Do effects that read "cannot be rested" mean that the affected Characters cannot attack?

**A:** Yes, the affected Characters cannot attack. These effects make the affected Characters unable to perform any actions that require them to be rested, such as attacking or activating [Blocker].

**Q:** Do effects that read "cannot be rested" mean that the affected Characters cannot activate effects such as "[Activate: Main] You may rest this Character:" and other similar effects?

**A:** Yes, the affected Characters cannot activate those effects. These effects make the affected Characters unable to perform any actions that require them to be rested, such as attacking or activating [Blocker].

**Q:** Do effects that read "cannot be rested" mean that the affected Characters cannot activate [Blocker]?

**A:** Yes, the affected Characters cannot activate [Blocker]. These effects make the affected Characters unable to perform any actions that require them to be rested, such as attacking or activating [Blocker].

**Q:** Do effects that read "cannot be rested" mean that the affected Characters cannot become rested by the effects of your or your opponent's other cards?

**A:** Yes, the affected Characters cannot be rested by the effects of other cards. These effects make the affected Characters unable to perform any actions that require them to be rested, such as attacking or activating [Blocker].
